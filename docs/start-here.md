# Start Here

This page gives beginners the shortest safe path into LUMINA.

By the end, two partners should understand enough to read and practice **CM-A**, the first core movement sequence.

## Safety first

LUMINA is choreographed movement. It is not full-contact sparring.

Before practicing:

- Use appropriate training sabers or safe props.
- Move slowly at first.
- Do not strike the body.
- Stop the saber before contact.
- Communicate clearly with your partner.
- Increase speed only after both partners understand the sequence.

## Core idea

A LUMINA exchange is written as a two-row table.

- **Lumen A** is usually the initiating partner.
- **Lumen B** responds.
- Each column represents one step.
- A number means an intended target.
- A number followed by `P` means a parry against that target.

## CM-A example

| Player | Step 1 | Step 2 | Step 3 | Step 4 | Step 5 | Step 6 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Lumen A | 1 | 11 | 3 | 9 | 5 | 7 |
| Lumen B | 1P | 11P | 3P | 9P | 5P | 7P |

In Step 1, Lumen A attacks target `1`, while Lumen B performs `1P`.
